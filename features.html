SPACEEXPLORER

A galaxy of blocks, credits, and one very angry machine at its center.

You start on the rim of a hostile galaxy with a small ship and 500 credits. Build your ship block by block, explore inward through ever-richer (and ever more dangerous) territory, trade and mine your way to a fortune, command a fleet that works for you even while you sleep — and finally breach the barrier at the core to destroy the Ravager and free the galaxy.

🧩 The Features

Nine interlocking systems. Every card lists what it feeds into — nothing exists in isolation.

🛠️ Ship Building

Design your ship on a grid, Lego-style. Every block matters: engines push, thrusters turn (further from the center = better leverage), generators power shields, cargo bays haul, crew quarters staff it all.

  • Drag-to-size blocks, mirror tool, 50-step undo
  • Turrets mount on hardpoints — bigger mounts fit bigger guns
  • Damage is per-block: lose your engines and you'll limp; lose your core and it's over
  • Up to 6 module slots grow with your hull size
Feeds into:CombatFleet (commissioning)Mining & cargo

🗺️ Exploration & the Galaxy Map

Fog of war covers the galaxy. Jump sector to sector, scan your surroundings, plot multi-jump routes that fly themselves, and shortcut across the galaxy through paired warp gates.

  • Flying is physical: your ship banks toward its course (big hulls lumber, light ones snap around), steers around asteroids and stations, and never ghosts through anything
  • Every station type looks like what it is — a rotating trade wheel, an open drydock with a half-built hull inside, greenhouse domes, a grimy tank-farm refinery
  • Sensors and scout captains widen what each jump reveals
  • Anomalies hide treasure maps, salvage, modules — or ambushes
  • Rare giant asteroids: sell the claim for fast cash, or found a permanent ore mine
  • Ancient battlefields drift in empty space — one-time debris fields of broken hulls nobody witnessed die, ready to strip for salvage
  • Station rumors point you to the Wardens' lairs
Feeds into:Trade routesFleet ordersEndgame (finding lairs)

⚔️ Combat

Battles are real-time, physical fights: shots strike individual blocks, shields form bubbles, missiles arc in and point-defense guns swat them down, carriers launch fighter swarms.

  • Plan first: stances, target priorities, formations, retreat thresholds
  • Command mid-fight: focus fire, change stance, retreat (retreat always gets you out — at a price)
  • Big rosters fight in waves of 10 per side
  • Crippled enemies surrender — capture the hull or scrap it
  • Wrecks remember what died there: return to a battle site and see the broken, charred hulls of the ships you destroyed — visibly eroding as you strip them for salvage
  • No two threats look alike: over a dozen pirate hull variants, half a dozen civilian freighter designs, and each faction's own dedicated warship classes
  • Every faction's ships read as themselves at a glance — Okkan hammerheads, Veyra lances, Solmar missile outriggers, Drex scrap-welds, Ithil carrier wings — with a subtle faction color accent on any hull, even captured ones
  • Every mounted laser fires its own visible beam — a four-laser miner carves rock with four beams, not one
  • Ambushes roll different compositions per sector — light swarms, artillery packs behind a screen, carrier groups, or a few hardened veterans
  • Rare Pirate Base HQs are ruled by named pirate lords on unique flagship hulls — read their dossier before you engage, beat them for guaranteed Legendary loot
Feeds into:Loot & wrecksReputationCaptured blueprints

💰 Economy & Trade

Six station types with living markets: prices move with stock, producers sell cheap, consumers pay well. Haul goods along the gradients, or let the trade ledger remember every price you've ever seen.

  • Refine mined ore into building materials (80% yield — or free onboard)
  • Craft turrets at equipment docks from recipes
  • Contraband pays 1.6× in faction space — if the patrols don't catch you
  • Build your own trade depots, factories, and asteroid mines; chain factories (machinery → electronics) for real production lines
Feeds into:Ship buildingFleet costsReputation

🚀 Fleet Command

Commission copies of your designs at shipyards, hire captains, and put the galaxy to work. Ships fly their orders for real — mine a field, run a trade loop, patrol a sector, escort you into battle.

  • 7 captain classes that level up: miners, traders, explorers, smugglers, hunters, commodores, industrialists
  • Command from the map or right in the sector — box-select a group, right-click a target, shift-queue chains of orders
  • Watch them work: your miners visibly fly rock to rock, beams and all
  • Set a miner to Auto-mine (roam) and it clears a field, then flies itself to the nearest sector that still has ore — hands-free forever
  • Miners manage their own cargo hold: fit a refinery and it drains ore into materials as it works, pausing mining only until there's room again; without one, a full hold sends it on an automatic supply run to the nearest refinery and back
  • Set a captained ship to Supply station… and it parks at a station, cashing in whatever material it's asked for at a cash premium — a standing courier for that station's request
  • Everything keeps running on other screens — and up to 4 hours while the game is closed
Feeds into:Economy (automation)Combat (escorts)Exploration (scouts)

🏛️ Factions & War

Five factions rule the mid-rings, and they don't get along. Wars break out, fronts ignite, and territory changes hands — with or without you. Pick a side at any front and tip the balance yourself.

  • Reputation from trading and pirate-hunting: better prices, patrol protection, allied warships
  • Sack a faction back to its core, then assault its capital — collapse it for good
  • Each faction sells an exclusive module to its best friends (rep 60+)
  • Go too far the other way and you're an outlaw: docks close, patrols shoot on sight
Feeds into:Economy (prices)Combat (allies/sieges)Smuggling

💎 Loot, Rarity & Research

Turrets and modules come in four qualities — Common to Legendary — and quality is real power. Battles drop better gear the deeper you fight; bosses guarantee Rare or better.

  • Over a dozen generic modules: shields, damage, speed, cargo, scanners, mining rigs, repair fields, jump range, crew and point-defense boosts…
  • Five faction-exclusive modules aren't just bigger numbers — each is a build-around effect: in-combat shield regen, an opening-salvo damage spike, sharper weapon tracking, doubled ramming damage, an extra fighter per bay
  • Research stations grind three spares into one of the next tier
  • Legendary gear only comes from combat — the market can't sell you the best
Feeds into:Ship powerCombat rewardsSieges (faction exclusives)

🎯 Objectives, Missions & QoL

A fifteen-step objective ladder guides you from your first mined rock to the final battle — early rungs, then a midgame band (found a mine, capture a ship, win a warfront, clear a Pirate Base HQ, reach the deep xenor ring), the Warden hunt, and the Ravager — each step paying a reward. Station bulletin boards offer rotating bounties and deliveries, while every station also keeps a standing material request you (or a fleet ship) can fetch for a premium. Story beats surface the galaxy's history as you hit milestones, and an in-game Codex explains every system.

  • Missions pay fair money — the faction reputation is the real prize
  • Station requests pay a straight cash premium over market price — no reputation, no deadline, just haul the material in and get paid; a fresh request rolls in the moment one's fulfilled
  • Story fragments — anomalies, Warden memories, the barrier, the throne — build toward an epilogue, re-readable in the Codex
  • The Codex also catalogues every ship and station you've encountered (stats + loadout unlock as you meet them) and a full turret reference table — plus a spoiler-safe gallery of every hull in the galaxy, browsable from the main menu before you've even started
  • Three save slots + export/import; start on a chosen galaxy seed, or unlock New Game+ after your first victory
  • New Game+ hands you a remixed galaxy — denser and tougher pirates, more anomalies to investigate, and extra Pirate Base HQs to storm — so a second run feels freshly dangerous, not a rerun (your original campaigns are untouched)
  • A living world: NPC traders on the lanes, wandering merchants, pirate raids you can choose to repel
Feeds into:OnboardingReputationEverything, gently

🌐 Multiplayer (self-hosted)

Play the same galaxy with friends. A friend runs a small self-hosted server; everyone else joins with an address — no accounts, no matchmaking service, no always-online requirement for singleplayer.

  • Cooperative shared galaxy: same sectors, wars, and stations for every connected player
  • Your real ship design is uploaded and validated server-side — no client can lie about their own stats
  • See other players' ships fly around live in a shared sector
  • Singleplayer stays 100% local — no server required unless you choose to connect
Feeds into:Ship building (blueprint sync)Exploration (shared galaxy)Factions & War (shared fronts)

👑 The Road to Victory

The whole game builds to this — a fully winnable campaign from your first iron rock to the Ravager's throne.
1Learn the rimMine, refine, trade, clear your first pirates. The objective ladder walks you through it.
2Build an empireCommission a fleet, hire captains, set up trade routes and stations that earn while you explore.
3Hunt the WardensFour distinct boss battleships — Vigil, Pharos, Tempest, and Maul, each with its own fighting doctrine and memory — guard the barrier artifacts in lairs near the core ring. Buy rumors to find them.
4Breach the barrierFour artifacts open the sealed core — voidstone territory, the galaxy's richest and deadliest ground.
5Storm the fortressThree staged waves at (0,0): shield spires, gun platforms and a carrier, then the Ravager itself.
6The remnant layerWinning isn't the end. Dormant Warden remnants and two apex superbosses — Hunter Alpha and the Warden Remnant — re-emerge across the cleared galaxy. Optional post-victory hunts, the hardest fights in the game, for legendary trophies.
